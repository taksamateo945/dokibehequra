Lynette Taylor, the wife of Hall of Fame linebacker Lawrence Taylor, was arrested Thursday and charged with domestic violence. 

According to TMZ Sports, Lynette Taylor was charged with one count of domestic violence and one count of resisting arrest after the former New York Giants pass-rusher alleged that she struck him in the back of the head.

Lynette was reportedly ordered by a judge to stay at least 500 feet away from L.T. and is not allowed to have contact of any kind with her husband.

Per TMZ Sports, Lynette denied the allegations against her, saying, "That's a lie. My husband is a 300-pound linebacker."

The couple reportedly has been married since 2007.

L.T. spent 13 NFL seasons with the G-Men and is tied for 13th on the league's all-time sacks list with 132.5.

He was a three-time NFL Defensive Player of the Year, and Pro-Football-Reference ranks him as the No. 17 player of all time in terms of Approximate Value.
